How to read the chart

The centre is the market (Nifty 50). Sectors normally rotate clockwise through these four stages:

Improving (top-left)

Still weaker than the market, but momentum has turned up — the potential next leaders.

→ Watchlist, early nibble

Leading (top-right)

Stronger than the market and still gaining — the current outperformers.

→ Ride the trend, add on dips

Weakening (bottom-right)

Still strong, but momentum is cooling — leaders that may be tiring.

→ Book some profit, tighten stops

Lagging (bottom-left)

Weaker than the market and still sliding — best left alone until they improve.

→ Avoid / stay out

How to use it (5 steps)

  1. Pick a view: Weekly for the big trend, Daily for a faster, noisier read.
  2. Look at the tails. A long, straight tail = a persistent move. A tail that hooks back means the sector is losing conviction.
  3. Favour sectors in Leading and those rotating up from Improving; be cautious on names sliding from Weakening toward Lagging.
  4. Hit ▶ Play or drag the slider to replay the last few months — direction matters more than the single dot.
  5. Click a sector on the left to see which of its stocks are in an uptrend (50-DMA > 200-DMA and price > 50-DMA), then do your own research before acting.

Frequently asked questions

What is a Relative Rotation Graph (RRG)?

RRG, created by Julius de Kempenaer, shows how a group of sectors are performing relative to a benchmark (the Nifty 50) and whether that relative performance is improving or worsening. Each sector is a single moving dot, so you can compare all sectors at once and see where money is rotating.

What do the two axes mean?

The horizontal axis (Trend / RS-Ratio) is how strong a sector is versus the Nifty — 100 = in line, above 100 = outperforming. The vertical axis (Momentum / RS-Momentum) is whether that strength is speeding up or slowing down — above 100 = gaining ground. The exact centre (100, 100) is the market itself.

What do the four quadrants mean?

Leading (top-right): stronger than the market and still improving. Weakening (bottom-right): still strong but momentum fading. Lagging (bottom-left): weak and still falling. Improving (top-left): weak but momentum turning up. Healthy markets rotate clockwise: Improving → Leading → Weakening → Lagging.

How do I read the tails?

Each dot drags a tail of its recent path. A long, straight tail means a steady, persistent move; a short or hooking tail means the sector is losing conviction and may change direction. The direction the tail points matters more than where the dot sits today.

What does "stocks in an uptrend" mean?

Click any sector to reveal the stocks inside it in a clear uptrend, using a classic trend filter: its 50-day moving average is above its 200-day moving average and the current price is above the 50-day average. Each chip also shows how far price is above its 50-DMA. This is an absolute price-trend check on the stock — separate from the sector's relative RRG position.

What are sub-sectors?

Broad sectors hide what's underneath. "Financials" splits into NBFCs, Insurance, Capital Markets and Housing Finance, which often rotate very differently. Switch the toggle to + Sub-sectors or pick a Group to bring in the finer indices (marked SUB). There are 45 indices in all — 17 broad sectors and 28 sub-sectors.

Daily or Weekly — which should I use?

Weekly smooths out day-to-day noise and suits swing/positional decisions — anchor here. Daily reacts faster but is noisier and easy to over-read. Form your view on Weekly, fine-tune on Daily.

Can I act on a single move into a quadrant?

Rarely. RRG shows the structural tide, not a precise entry. A one-period hop across a line is often noise. Look for a sector travelling consistently in one direction over several periods, then confirm with the leading stocks inside it. Treat RRG as a map, not a trade signal.

How is each sector index built and how fresh is the data?

Each sector is a cap-weighted basket of its real constituent stocks, just like the official NSE sectoral indices. Prices are end-of-session daily data; the "updated" date in the header shows the latest session. The page rebuilds automatically every weekday evening.

Is this investment advice?

No. This is an educational and research tool to understand market structure and sector trends. It does not account for your goals, risk or position sizing, and is not investment advice.
